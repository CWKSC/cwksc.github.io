## Problem 3.8(b)

Assume a uniform prior over $\pi$. Use the identity

$$
\int_0^1 \pi^m (1-\pi)^n d\pi = \frac{m!n!}{(m+n+1)!} \quad (3.32)
$$

to show that

$$
p(\pi|\mathcal{D}) = \frac{(n+1)!}{s!(n-s)!}\pi^s (1-\pi)^{n-s}. \quad (3.33)
$$

Plot this density for $n=1$ for each value of $s$.
